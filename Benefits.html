Getting Started

Pilates exercise began with mat. Some clients prefer learning mat first or, in addition to equipment exercise. Mat classes are mostley performed on the floor, sometimes utilizing Pilates props, such as the magic circles, foam roller, balls, and therabands.

For those unfamiliar with Pilates equipment, a fitness/postural evaluation is necessary. Private lessons offer the entire range of equipment including the cadillac, reformer, wunda chair, and props.

Duets and Trios are done on the reformer. Flexible and comfortable clothing should be worn.

Benefits

Regular Pilates clients taught by an experienced instructor, notice the following benefits:

  • Longer, leaner, toned muscles
  • Improved posture and Balance
  • Increased core strength, granting an new, more integrated physical power
  • Increased range of motion and flexibility
  • Increased energy level and improved circulation
  • Greater stability in hyper-mobile joints
  • Improved sports performance (tennis, golf, running, skiing, swimming, etc.)
  • More balanced body (most people have a stronger side)
  • Adaptive method, able to suit the injured, pregnant, dancers, athletes, seniors, and beginners
